What’s a pitch?
• A PR pitch is a short, personalized message that outlines the value of a story
and explains why it should be published.
• PR pitches should be short, engaging and timely for the topic.
• But pitching isn’t easy.
• With fewer journalists (there are nearly six PR pros for every one
journalist), faster news cycles and an unprecedented amount of media
outlets, communicators need to get savvier and more strategic about how
they tell stories.
• It’s no secret that PR pros can’t do their jobs effectively without
journalists, and journalists rely on PR pros, too.

Importance of a Strong Pitch
• Capturing Media Attention
 -Highlights news worthiness but also shows you understand a journalists interest
• Building Relationships with Journalists
 -Opportunity to build and strengthen trust
• Amplifying an Organization’s Message

Key Elements of a Strong Pitch
• Newsworthiness (Identify the story)
• Relevance (research the journalist and outlet)
• Clarity and Conciseness (craft the pitch)
• Engaging Subject Line
• Compelling Hook (in the lead)
 -Personalize the pitch
• Supporting Information
• Call to Action
 -Event, offer for an interview, etc.
• Follow Up

4 essential guidelines for the perfect pitch in 2021

1. Pitch length:
91% of journalists prefer pitches under 200 words. Nearly half (46%) prefer 1-200 words and a quarter (25%) prefer under 100 words.

2. When to pitch:
68% of journalists prefer to receive pitches between 5 AM and 12 PM EST. Journalists again cited Monday as their favorite day to receive pitches (57%), followed by Tuesday
(20%).

3. On follow ups:
86% of journalists are OK with a follow up email within one week of the initial message.
The other 14% prefer a follow up over 1 week from the initial pitch. 90% of journalists say it's OK to send at least 1 follow up email; 38% say 2 or more.

4. Pitching Channels:

- 1:1 Email: 94% Like, 8% doesn't like
- Mass email/ newswire: 18% like, 37% does not like
- Phone: 10% like, 66% does not like
- Twitter: 12% like, 48% does not like
- Other Social Networks: 9% like, 54% does not like
- Other: 4% like, 4% does not like

Pitches
We all have great story ideas. Getting reporters, editors or webmasters to agree with that can be challenging. They receive hundreds of releases and pitches daily, and they don't have the time or resources to vet everything that comes to their inbox or voicemail.
A pitch needs to be more like a trailer than the movie. An effective pitch is a teaser that leaves the reporter or editor wanting to hear more. It is short like an ad, concise like a tweet, and packed with a punch - the "so what" that every reporter wants for their audience.
Like every other tactic we've mentioned, a pitch is also strategic.
Pitching a reporter is not like throwing out a fishing net; it's a focused communication act in which your objectives and strategies are crystal clear. You know exactly which reporter will help you achieve your objec-tixe, and you know how to convince the reporter that it's newsworthy.
• Focus on its news value. Why should the reporter cover this topic now?
• Make it short and to the point. Try drafting your pitch by writing a weet. If you can't get the most compelling points out in 280 charac-ters, try again.
• Add value. What else can you offer the reporter that will enhance the story? This could be a rare interview with an executive, a unique opportunity to get behind-the-scenes at a manufacturing facility, or a chance to talk with beneficiaries of your community service proj-ects. Don't forget the photographs and videos.
• Ractice. Give your pitch to a friend or colleague and ask for honest feedback. You don't want a pitch to be scripted, but rehearsing it will make you more comfortable with the details and help you deliver it in your own words.
• Don't overpromise. Never offer a reporter more than you can give, and don't oversell your pitch with unsubstantiated claims.
• Be prepared. Finish assembling all materials for the media and get internal approvals in advance of giving your pitch. Offer materials. such as a news release, images, videos or an interview list, to the reporter the moment they ask for more information.
• Go back to the beginning. Have you already established a relationship with your target media? How did they say they wanted to be contacted?
