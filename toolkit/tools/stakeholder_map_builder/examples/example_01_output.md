# Example 01 — Output: AI Regulation (Federal)

## Issue Overview
The artificial intelligence regulation landscape features a complex coalition of tech companies, civil society organizations, and legislators. Large technology companies are generally supportive of voluntary frameworks while opposing mandatory requirements. Academic and safety-focused nonprofits predominantly support stricter federal oversight.

## Proponents (8)
*AI safety advocates and legislators pushing for federal oversight dominate the proponent coalition.*

- **Center for AI Safety** (Nonprofit) — Filed LDA on "artificial intelligence safety standards" (2024 Q2) *[high influence]*
- **Sen. Amy Klobuchar** (Legislator, MN) — Primary sponsor of AI transparency bill S.2123 *[high influence]*
- **Rep. Ted Lieu** (Legislator, CA) — Co-sponsored multiple AI accountability bills *[medium influence]*

## Opponents (4)
*Industry groups opposing mandatory disclosure and liability requirements.*

- **Chamber of Commerce** (Coalition) — Lobbied against AI liability provisions; LDA filing $2,400,000 (2024) *[high influence]*
- **TechNet** (Coalition) — LDA filing "artificial intelligence" issues, $850,000 (2024 Q3) *[medium influence]*

## Neutral / Unknown (12)
- **Microsoft Corporation** — Active in AI governance discussions; stance on specific mandates unclear
- **Google LLC** — Supports voluntary AI principles; mixed record on federal mandates

## Key Relationships
- **Akin Gump Strauss** lobbies for **Microsoft Corporation** (2024 Q3)
- **Sen. Klobuchar** co-sponsors AI Safety Act with **Rep. Lieu**

## Strategic Notes
The landscape is fluid with several high-influence corporations publicly supporting AI safety principles while lobbying against specific mandates — a potential wedge issue. Legislators are mostly on the coasts with bipartisan interest but no unified bill.
