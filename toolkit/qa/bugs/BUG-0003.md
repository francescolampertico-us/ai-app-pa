## Observed Behavior
The memo appears to rely on a very small number of recent articles and disclosures, then overweights whichever details happen to be in those few sources. In practice, `Overview of Activities` centers too heavily on NATO Days and even includes low-priority operational detail about flood disruption, instead of giving a broader plain-English picture of Jagello 2000's activities and role.

## Expected Behavior
The Background Memo should synthesize a broader research base and prioritize the most decision-relevant facts. For an organization like Jagello 2000, the memo should not read like a recap of a handful of recent articles; it should explain the entity's mission, major programs, positioning, network, and relevance in a clear backgrounder style.

## Why This Matters
This is a core quality failure for the tool. Even if the facts are technically sourced, a memo that overfits to too few articles and surfaces low-value details will mislead users about what matters most and make the output feel cluttered and strategically weak.

## Suspected Layer
`toolkit`

## Claude Fix Request
Improve the Background Memo research-and-synthesis pipeline so it uses a broader and better-prioritized research base for memo generation. Revisit the article cap and synthesis strategy so the memo does not overfit to a few recent articles or elevate low-value operational details above core organizational background. Preserve concise output, but make it clearer, broader, and more strategically prioritized.
