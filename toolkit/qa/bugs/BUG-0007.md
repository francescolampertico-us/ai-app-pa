## Observed Behavior
The overall briefing is strategically useful, but the disclosure section includes broad lobbying activity on related topics that does not clearly help brief Sen. Cantwell herself. This creates noise and makes the briefing feel less targeted.

## Expected Behavior
The disclosure section should include only disclosure information that is directly relevant to understanding the stakeholder, their ecosystem, or the immediate meeting context. Topic-adjacent lobbying traffic should be filtered, summarized more selectively, or omitted when it does not improve the briefing.

## Why This Matters
Stakeholder Briefing is supposed to help meeting preparation. If the disclosure section adds loosely related activity instead of stakeholder-useful insight, it increases reading load and reduces trust in the briefing.

## Evidence / Output Excerpt
Example of noisy disclosure entries from the generated briefing:

```text
COMMON CAUSE ... Voting & Elections Bills in the 119th Congress ...
LAWYERS' COMMITTEE FOR CIVIL RIGHTS UNDER LAW ... Opposing the ...
```

These may be valid lobbying records, but they do not obviously improve a briefing for a meeting with Sen. Cantwell on an AI testing and transparency framework.

## Suspected Layer
`toolkit`

## Claude Fix Request
Improve the Stakeholder Briefing disclosure-selection logic so the disclosure section favors information that is directly useful for briefing the named stakeholder and meeting objective. Reduce noisy topic-adjacent disclosure items that are technically related to the broader policy area but not decision-useful for the meeting.
