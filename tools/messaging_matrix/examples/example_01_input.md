# Example 1: AI Safety Legislation

## Position
Support the AI Safety and Innovation Act (S.1234) — mandatory pre-deployment safety testing for frontier AI models protects consumers and national security without stifling American innovation leadership.

## Context
The AI Safety and Innovation Act (S.1234), introduced by Sen. Warner (D-VA) and Sen. Young (R-IN), would require companies developing frontier AI models above a compute threshold to conduct pre-deployment safety evaluations and report results to NIST. The bill passed the Senate Commerce Committee on a 19-7 bipartisan vote. Key provisions include: mandatory red-teaming for models above 10^26 FLOP, a NIST AI Safety Institute to develop evaluation standards, liability safe harbor for companies that comply with testing requirements, and a $500M authorization for AI safety research. Industry groups including the Information Technology Industry Council have expressed cautious support, while some civil liberties organizations have raised concerns about the compute threshold being too narrow.
