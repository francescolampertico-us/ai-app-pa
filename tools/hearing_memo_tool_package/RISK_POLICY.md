# Risk Policy - Congressional Hearing Memo Tool

## 1. Purpose

This tool produces professional memo outputs that may be circulated internally or adapted for client use. Because the output can influence public affairs analysis and external communications, the tool must operate under explicit review rules.

## 2. Risk levels

### Green
Low-risk internal drafting support.
Examples:
- transcript cleanup
- witness list normalization
- section-heading selection
- rough extraction JSON

Human review: recommended

### Yellow
Professional internal memo draft.
Examples:
- polished hearing summary memo
- same-day internal readout
- committee recap circulated to policy team

Human review: mandatory before distribution

### Red
External-facing or high-consequence use.
Examples:
- client-ready memo sent without analyst review
- publication-ready commentary
- outputs making legal, regulatory, or market-sensitive claims

Human review: mandatory, with subject-matter signoff

## 3. Never-do rules

The tool must never:
- invent names, titles, affiliations, or dates
- fabricate legislative or policy significance
- present uncertainty as fact
- claim consensus without evidence
- omit known metadata conflicts silently
- guess missing witness identity from context alone
- generate final external copy without human approval

## 4. Required checks before a memo can be approved

Reviewer must check:
- hearing title
- committee / subcommittee
- hearing date and time
- memo date
- witness names and affiliations
- section completeness
- major factual claims
- any quoted language
- confidentiality footer

## 5. Escalation triggers

Escalate for manual handling if:
- transcript is incomplete or obviously corrupted
- metadata sources conflict
- witness names are missing or ambiguous
- hearing format is unusual and does not fit the approved section family
- major claims cannot be grounded in source text

## 6. Approval note

All finished memos are draft outputs until a human reviewer signs off.
